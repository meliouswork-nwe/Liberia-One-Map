Available Datasets

National Geospatial Data Layers

Authoritative datasets covering geodetic control, administrative boundaries, infrastructure, social facilities, and satellite imagery — all in LibRef21 (EPSG:10800), Liberia's official national datum.

📍
LGREF — Geodetic Monuments
27 horizontal and vertical control monuments from the Liberia Infrastructure Data Portal (LIDP). Includes the LLA-CORS station at Fendell Campus. Full LibRef21 geographic and UTM Zone 29N coordinates for every monument.
27 monuments 11 counties LIVE
🗺
County Boundaries
All 15 Liberia county administrative boundaries. Source: OCHA COD-AB / LISGIS / ITOS-USAID. Authoritative for government, UN, World Bank, and donor program use. Valid 4 November 2019.
15 counties OCHA COD-AB LIVE
🏥
Health Facilities
National health facility coverage from Healthsites.io. Includes clinics, hospitals, pharmacies, and specialised facilities as both point locations and building polygons. Source: Healthsites · OSM.
854 points 321 polygons LIVE
🎓
Education Facilities
Schools, colleges, universities, and kindergartens nationwide. Mapped as point locations and building footprints. Source: HOTOSM / OSM field survey data.
245 points 1,751 polygons LIVE
✈️
Transport Infrastructure
Railways, airports, ports and harbours, border crossings, public transport stops, pipelines, and parking areas. National coverage from NextGIS / OpenStreetMap.
9 layer types NextGIS · OSM LIVE
💧
Water Resources
Water bodies, water points, and wells across Liberia. Simplified national coverage available now. Detailed waterway and water polygon layers being prepared per county.
Water bodies Water points LIVE
🌿
Environment & Nature
Nature reserves and protected areas, islands, and ESA WorldCover 2021 land cover overlay (10m resolution, 11 classes). Free CC-BY 4.0 from ESA / Terrascope WMS.
Nature reserves ESA WorldCover LIVE
📡
Bomi County Pilot
Full 7-layer vector dataset for Bomi County — the first complete county pilot. Roads, rivers, water bodies, land use, buildings, and PMO survey boundary. Client: BomiCo PMO.

National road network (secondary and tertiary) and land use classification being prepared per county. Large-file datasets being county-cropped for efficient web delivery.

Using the Portal

How LrOneMap Works

A single-page web map designed for geospatial professionals, planners, and engineers. No software to install — runs entirely in your browser.

1
Open the Map
Click "Open the Map" to launch the full-screen interactive portal. Select your basemap — OpenStreetMap, ESRI Satellite, or Topographic.
2
Toggle Layers
Use the left panel to turn national data layers on and off — monuments, boundaries, health, education, transport, and more.
3
Select a County
Go to the LGMAP tab and select any of the 15 counties from the dropdown to zoom and load that county's available data layers.
4
Click Features
Click any feature on the map for a popup with full LibRef21 coordinates, attributes, and a direct link to Google Satellite imagery.
5
Switch Coordinates
Toggle between LibRef21 Geographic (EPSG:10800) and UTM Zone 29N (EPSG:10802) in the status bar at the bottom of the map.
6
Download Data
Access the Downloads tab in the portal for GeoJSON and KMZ files of geodetic monuments, county boundaries, and available datasets.
About This Portal

Liberia Geospatial Data Infrastructure

LrOneMap.com, the Liberia OneMap, is a Liberia National Geospatial Data Repository — currently a Demonstration Operational Prototype (DOP) for the Liberia Geospatial Data Infrastructure (LGDI). This portal is designed to provide open access to authoritative Geospatial Reference data, Base Mapping data, DTM/DEM, and Imagery data in support of Geospatial and AEC professionals, including Photogrammetric Engineers, Survey Engineers, Environmental Engineers, and Planners for Infrastructure projects, Cadastral Surveys, and Private/Public Investment initiatives across Liberia.
